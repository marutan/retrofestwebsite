UNIX Machines at RetroFest 2025

Here are the machines you can (hopefully) see in our little UNIX corner of RetroFest 2025. Apologies if any of these are missing - they are very old and fragile and I fear some might not survive the weekend.

Silicon Graphics Power Indigo 2

The Silicon Graphics Indigo 2 replaced the earlier Indigo desktop and Silicon Graphic's mid-range single-processor workstation. It launched with the MIPS R4000 in 1992 in a distinctive teal coloured case, later upgraded to the MIPS R4400.

MIPS realised that the R4400 was getting beaten by its competition, especially at floating point, and so they produced the 64-bit MIPS R8000. This monster of a processor cost $2,5000 at launch in June 1994 (never mind the rest of the computer) and shipped as a PCB covered in many large (and hot) ICs. It worked though, and by only November 1994, 50 of the world's Top 500 fastest computers used the R8000 processor. Despite clocking at only 75 MHz, its architecture allowed it to perform up to 300 million instructions per second.

An Indigo 2 equipped with a 75 MHz MIPS R8000 is known as a "Power Indigo 2" and they were only sold for about 18 months before being replaced by the MIPS R10K based Indigo 2 (which have purple cases). Prices are hard to find, but I believe the Power Indigo 2 cost around $85,000 when new, making them exceptionally rare. Enjoy using this one then (and let's hope it lasts the weekend...)
